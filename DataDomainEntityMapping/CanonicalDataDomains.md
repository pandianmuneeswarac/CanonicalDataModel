# Canonical Data Domains

## Enterprise Banking Data Domain Definitions

| Serial Number | Data Domain Name | Definition |
|---------------|------------------|------------|
| 1 | Customer | Encompasses all data related to individual retail customers, corporate clients, and institutional entities including demographics, identification, relationships, preferences, and customer lifecycle information. This domain serves as the single source of truth for party identification across all banking channels and products. Includes KYC/AML compliance data, customer segmentation, and cross-channel customer behavior analytics. |
| 2 | Account | Contains comprehensive data about all financial account types including deposit accounts (checking, savings, money market), loan accounts, credit facilities, and investment accounts. Covers account structure, ownership, balances, status, terms and conditions, interest rates, fees, and account lifecycle management. Includes account hierarchies, beneficial ownership structures, and multi-currency account representations. |
| 3 | Transaction | Captures all financial movements and activities across accounts including deposits, withdrawals, transfers, payments, debits, credits, and adjustments. Encompasses transaction details such as amount, currency, timestamp, channel, authorization status, settlement information, and transaction categorization. Includes both posted and pending transactions with full audit trail and reconciliation data. |
| 4 | Product | Defines banking product catalog including product specifications, features, pricing structures, eligibility criteria, terms and conditions, and product lifecycle stages. Covers retail banking products, commercial lending products, investment products, insurance products, and fee-based services. Includes product bundling rules, cross-sell/up-sell relationships, and product profitability metrics. |
| 5 | Party | Represents all entities that can have relationships with the bank including individuals, organizations, legal entities, government bodies, and third-party service providers. Covers party roles, relationships, hierarchies, legal structures, and party identification across multiple systems. Includes beneficial ownership, corporate structures, and party network relationships for regulatory reporting. |
| 6 | Agreement | Contains contractual arrangements between the bank and parties including loan agreements, credit facilities, service agreements, custody agreements, and master service agreements. Covers terms, conditions, covenants, collateral requirements, pricing schedules, and agreement lifecycle management. Includes amendment history, renewal terms, and cross-default provisions. |
| 7 | Collateral | Encompasses all assets pledged as security for credit facilities including real estate, securities, cash deposits, equipment, inventory, receivables, and guarantees. Covers collateral valuation, ownership verification, lien positions, insurance requirements, and collateral monitoring. Includes collateral allocation across multiple obligations and collateral substitution rules. |
| 8 | Risk | Contains data related to credit risk, market risk, operational risk, liquidity risk, and compliance risk including risk ratings, probability of default, loss given default, exposure at default, risk limits, and risk mitigation strategies. Covers stress testing scenarios, risk concentration analysis, and regulatory capital calculations. Includes early warning indicators and risk appetite framework metrics. |
| 9 | Payment | Captures payment instructions, payment processing details, payment routing information, and payment settlement data across all payment channels including wire transfers, ACH, real-time payments, checks, and card payments. Covers payment validation, fraud detection, sanctions screening, and payment status tracking. Includes correspondent banking relationships and payment network connectivity. |
| 10 | Regulatory Reporting | Contains data required for regulatory compliance and reporting including capital adequacy reports, liquidity coverage ratios, stress test submissions, anti-money laundering reports, and consumer protection disclosures. Covers data lineage, calculation methodologies, report submissions, and regulatory correspondence. Includes audit trails for regulatory examinations and data quality certifications. |
| 11 | Channel | Encompasses customer interaction channels including branch banking, online banking, mobile banking, ATM networks, contact centers, and relationship manager interactions. Covers channel preferences, channel performance metrics, omnichannel customer journeys, and channel-specific transaction capabilities. Includes channel authentication methods and channel availability schedules. |
| 12 | Financial Market Data | Contains market prices, exchange rates, interest rate curves, credit spreads, volatility surfaces, and reference data for securities, derivatives, commodities, and foreign exchange. Covers market data sources, pricing methodologies, valuation adjustments, and market data quality controls. Includes end-of-day pricing, intraday pricing, and historical market data for risk analytics. |
| 13 | Asset | Represents bank-owned assets including investment securities, trading portfolios, real estate holdings, equipment, intellectual property, and other tangible and intangible assets. Covers asset classification, valuation, depreciation, impairment testing, and asset lifecycle management. Includes asset performance tracking and asset disposition processes. |
| 14 | Liability | Contains data about bank obligations including deposits, borrowings, debt securities issued, derivatives liabilities, and other financial obligations. Covers liability terms, maturity profiles, interest rate characteristics, and liability management strategies. Includes funding sources, liquidity buffers, and liability repricing schedules. |
| 15 | General Ledger | Encompasses the complete chart of accounts, journal entries, account balances, financial statements, and accounting policies. Covers double-entry bookkeeping, period-end close processes, inter-company eliminations, and financial consolidation. Includes sub-ledger reconciliations, accounting adjustments, and audit trail for all financial postings. |
