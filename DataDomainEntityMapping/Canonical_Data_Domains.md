# Canonical Data Domains

## Complete List of Data Domains with Definitions

| Serial Number | Data Domain Name | Definition |
|---------------|------------------|------------|
| 1 | Customer | Encompasses all data related to individual retail customers, corporate clients, and prospects including demographic information, relationships, preferences, and customer lifecycle management across all banking channels and products. |
| 2 | Account | Contains comprehensive data about all financial accounts including deposit accounts (checking, savings, money market), loan accounts, credit facilities, and investment accounts with their balances, statuses, terms, and account-level attributes. |
| 3 | Party | Represents all legal entities and individuals that can have relationships with the bank including customers, non-customers, beneficiaries, guarantors, co-borrowers, and third parties with their identification, classification, and relationship data. |
| 4 | Product | Defines all banking products and services offered across retail, commercial, and wealth management including product specifications, features, pricing structures, eligibility criteria, and product lifecycle information. |
| 5 | Transaction | Captures all financial transactions across all channels including deposits, withdrawals, transfers, payments, card transactions, and fee assessments with complete transaction details, posting information, and audit trails. |
| 6 | Card | Manages all card-related data including credit cards, debit cards, prepaid cards, and virtual cards with card attributes, limits, controls, rewards programs, and card lifecycle management information. |
| 7 | Loan | Contains comprehensive loan and credit facility data including mortgages, personal loans, auto loans, commercial loans, lines of credit with origination details, terms, collateral, repayment schedules, and loan performance metrics. |
| 8 | Collateral | Encompasses all collateral and security data pledged against credit facilities including real estate, securities, cash deposits, equipment, and other assets with valuation, lien information, and collateral management details. |
| 9 | Payment | Manages all payment-related data including wire transfers, ACH transactions, bill payments, peer-to-peer payments, international remittances, and payment instructions with routing, beneficiary, and settlement information. |
| 10 | Risk | Contains all risk management data including credit risk, market risk, operational risk, liquidity risk assessments, risk ratings, exposure calculations, stress testing results, and risk mitigation strategies. |
| 11 | Compliance | Encompasses regulatory compliance data including KYC (Know Your Customer), AML (Anti-Money Laundering), sanctions screening, regulatory reporting, audit trails, and compliance monitoring information. |
| 12 | Investment | Manages investment portfolio data including securities holdings, mutual funds, bonds, equities, alternative investments, asset allocation, performance metrics, and investment advisory information. |
| 13 | Channel | Contains data about all customer interaction channels including branch banking, online banking, mobile banking, ATM, call center, and relationship manager interactions with channel preferences and usage patterns. |
| 14 | Limit | Manages all types of limits including credit limits, transaction limits, exposure limits, concentration limits, and regulatory limits with limit structures, utilization tracking, and limit management workflows. |
| 15 | Fee | Contains comprehensive fee and charge data including account fees, transaction fees, service charges, penalty fees, and commission structures with fee schedules, waivers, and fee income tracking. |
| 16 | Interest | Manages all interest-related data including interest rates, rate indices, accrual calculations, interest income and expense, rate change history, and interest payment schedules across all products. |
| 17 | Document | Encompasses all document and content management data including customer documents, loan documents, account opening forms, statements, disclosures, contracts, and digital document metadata with version control. |
| 18 | Organization | Contains internal organizational data including branch hierarchy, business units, departments, cost centers, profit centers, legal entities, and organizational relationships for internal management and reporting. |
| 19 | Employee | Manages employee and workforce data including staff information, roles, responsibilities, relationship managers, sales officers, and employee-customer relationships with performance and attribution data. |
| 20 | Location | Contains geographic and location data including branch locations, ATM locations, customer addresses, property locations, and geographic hierarchies (country, state, city, postal code) for all location-based analytics. |
| 21 | Treasury | Manages treasury operations data including liquidity management, funding, capital markets activities, foreign exchange transactions, derivatives, hedging activities, and balance sheet management information. |
| 22 | Pricing | Contains pricing data for all products and services including rate sheets, pricing tiers, promotional rates, negotiated pricing, pricing rules, and pricing approval workflows across all business lines. |
| 23 | Event | Captures significant business events and milestones including account opening/closing, loan origination/payoff, customer lifecycle events, product changes, and other material events with event metadata and timestamps. |
| 24 | Reference | Contains all reference and master data including currency codes, country codes, product codes, transaction type codes, status codes, and other standardized reference data used across the enterprise. |
| 25 | Analytics | Encompasses derived analytical data including customer segmentation, propensity scores, risk scores, profitability metrics, predictive models, and business intelligence metrics used for decision-making and insights. |
